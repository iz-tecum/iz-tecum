Representation of the Riemann Sphere

2025 · December

Hello! I wanted to draw a clean-ish picture of the Riemann sphere projected onto the plane. In this model, \(\infty\) is adjoined to the otherwise complete \(\mathbb{C}\).

When Möbius maps start feeling overwhelming, I come back to this picture to reset intuition.

This post is also a small test of “math on the web”: CSS + JavaScript + KaTeX + SVG, stitched together into one page.

riemann sphere
stereographic projection
static diagram
Formally, we think of \(\mathbb{C}_\infty = \mathbb{C} \cup \{\infty\}\) as a sphere. The point \(\infty\) is the north pole in this picture.
Soft colors are just a visual cue: blue lives on the sphere side, green lives on the plane side, and the red line is the projection path.
When \(f = u + iv\) is holomorphic we get the usual Cauchy–Riemann conditions: \(\frac{\partial u}{\partial x} = \frac{\partial v}{\partial y}\) and \(\frac{\partial u}{\partial y} = - \frac{\partial v}{\partial x}\).
