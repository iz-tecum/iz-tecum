extra

With Lars Ahlfors on the left-hand side of the screen, it would be appropriate to say that complex analysis has been the most “complete” math experience I’ve had so far — it’s one of the first times a subject felt simultaneously geometric, analytic, and algebraic in a way that actually closes. The core ideas are deceptively simple, but the consequences are pretty cool. For example, I have observed behavior determining global structure, and integrals turning into exact information.

It’s the kind of course that changes how you write proofs and how you see functions. For those interested, Professor Tang-Kai Lee’s course notes for Honors Complex Variables can be found here. I also really appreciated his acknowledgement at the end. Incredible instructor.
